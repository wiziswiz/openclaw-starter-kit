# Company Research

## Standard Research Flow

### Step 1: Basic Info (web_search)
- Company name, founding year, HQ location
- Product description in 1-2 sentences
- Funding status / last round
- Notable investors or accelerator participation

### Step 2: Founders (web_search + web_fetch)
- Names and titles
- LinkedIn URLs
- Notable background (previous companies, education)

### Step 3: Social Presence
- Twitter/X handles for company AND founders
- Recent posts (engagement hooks for personalization)
- Any public discussions about partnerships, growth, challenges

### Step 4: Connection Hooks
- Mutual connections (check your network)
- Shared investors or advisors
- Geographic proximity
- Relevant recent announcements

### Step 5: Output Brief

```markdown
## [Company Name]
- **What:** [1-line product description]
- **Founders:** [Names] — [backgrounds]
- **Stage/Funding:** [round, amount, investors]
- **HQ:** [location]
- **Twitter:** [@handle]
- **Hook:** [personalization angle — reference their specific product, news, or challenge]

### Fit Assessment
- **Strongest angle:** [how your work/product/offer aligns with their needs]
- **Recommended approach:** [how to position, what to lead with]
- **Avoid:** [what NOT to pitch and why]

### Gaps / Open Questions
- [What we couldn't find]
- [What to ask in first conversation]
```

## Rules
1. **Hooks > facts** — the personalization angle is more valuable than a Wikipedia summary
2. **Parallelize gathering** — web, social, and internal data can run simultaneously
3. **Research without a recommended angle is incomplete** — always include fit assessment
4. **Quality over volume** — a tight 10-line brief beats a sprawling 3-page dossier
